Suffix Array

8 January 2021

Let $s$ be a string of length $n$. The $i$-th suffix of $s$ is the substring $s[i...n-1]$. The suffix array will contain integers that represent the starting indeces of all the suffixes of a given string, after the suffixes are sorted. For example, consider the string $s = abaab$. All suffixes are as follows $$[[0]abaab, [1]baab, [2]aab, [3]ab, [4]b].$$ After sorting these strings the arrangement is now $$[[2]aab, [3]ab, [0]abaab, [4]b, [1]baab]].$$ Therefore, the suffix array for $s$ will be $$[2, 3, 0, 4, 1].$$ As a data structure, suffix arrays are widely used in data compression, bionformatics, and any other areas that deal with strings and string matching.

$O(n^2\log n)$ Approach

This is the most naive approach. We sort the suffixes while retaining their original inices. Sorting with either mergesort or quicksort takes $O(n\log n)$, and since comparing two strings takes an additional $O(n)$ time, we get a final complexity of $O(n^2\log n)$.
