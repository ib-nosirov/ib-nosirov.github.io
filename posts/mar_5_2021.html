Proof Dissection

Prove that there is not rational number whose square root is $12$.


Proof: Suppose $m^2 = 12n^2$, where $m$ and $n$ have no common factor. It follows that $m$ must be even, and therefore $n$ must be odd. Let $m = 2r$. Then we have $r^2 = 3n^2$, so that $r$ is also odd. Let $r = 2s + 1$ and $n = 2t + 1$. Then $$4s^2 + 4s + 1 = 3(4t^2 + 4t + 1) = 12t^2 + 12t + 3,$$ so that $$4(s^2 + s - 3t^2 - 3t) = 2.$$ But this is absurd since $2$ cannot be a multiple of $4$.$\tag*{$\square$}$

This proof wastes no words while also having a flow to it. Most importantly, it makes sense, something that I've been struggling with in my own proofs. That shift from the first sentence to the second is the perfect logical leap — we know that $m$ must be even because otherwise it must be odd and that can't be the case since 12 contains a 2 and the equality would break down as an odd number can't contain a 2. This step forces the reader to think, but not assume anything other than the definitions of odd and even. I hope to get this good at proofs by the start of the fall semester, when I'm hoping to take real analysis. To do this, I need to work problems from Rudin and, more importantly, analyze them every day.
