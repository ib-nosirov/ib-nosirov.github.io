A bit about me, thoughts on learning, and the empty articles on the main page

Hi! I'm Ibrohim, a freshman in college majoring in math and computer science. I was born in Uzbekistan and moved to the US a week before my ninth birthday. I had only a vague notion of what a computer was, and math was a series of unconnected abstractions. I was further fortunate to have a series of excellent teachers. I particularly remember Ms. Whitney, a masterclass in emotional intelligence. Though their reasons might have been unique, all of my teachers were emotionally present in their students' school lives, and that, to me, matters more than course content.

I went to school in one of the wealthiest cities in the country while living on government subsidy. It was no fun at the time, but I came away with insight into the power each of us wields over the lives of others. Perhaps the most pertinent is the lesson in being present; learning is an emotional exercise, and our success has nothing to do with money. This type of success largely depends on the emotional intelligence of those around us. Try as they may, teachers often struggle to get close to each of their hundreds of students. I believe the answer is in the students themselves. We're no experts on pedagogy, but there is something to be said for the unspoken bond between two people trying to figure something out together. Combined with more dialogue around a growth mindset, this can change the whole culture around learning.

For most of my life, I thought of serving others as a dichotomy between those who make careers out of non-profit work and billionaires with nowhere left to go on the Hedonic treadmill but toward immortality through virtue. The past 43 weeks have shown me that lasting change comes from driven individuals, with families and careers, incessantly working toward the goal. Our desire to give back doesn't have to, and for the most part, will not cross with our passions and careers.

With everyone locked down, I've been able to work with peers in Uzbekistan. I was pleasantly surprised to see a persistent interest in shared learning. I want this collaborative spirit to continue long after restrictions lift. These conversations should not only inform but also inspire all of us to grow together. I've back-dated some of the insightful sessions I've had during the pandemic, first in Computer Science, and will be filling in and translating them throughout the month.
